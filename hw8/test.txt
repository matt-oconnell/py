<p class="story-body-text story-content" data-para-count="256" data-total-count="3205" id="story-continues-6">Comey tried to wriggle out of the charge that he had publicly disclosed the reopening of the Clinton investigation, saying he had sent a private letter to the lawmakers about it. But that letter became public instantly, as Comey had to know it would.</p><p class="story-body-text story-content" data-para-count="248" data-total-count="3453">Comey said he and his aides debated whether to inform Congress about the search warrant, with an election 11 days way. Contemplating the possibility of influencing that vote, <a href="https://www.usatoday.com/story/news/politics/onpolitics/2017/05/03/james-comey-testimony-quotes/101243978/">Comey said</a> he thought at the time, Lordy, that would be really bad.</p><p class="story-body-text story-content" data-para-count="291" data-total-count="3744">But he thought that not revealing the new investigation would be catastrophic. Its not clear what catastrophe he had in mind, other than Clinton being elected while the F.B.I. was still looking at the emails, which they miraculously managed to finish doing two days before the vote.</p><button class="button comments-button  theme-speech-bubble-large" data-skip-to-para-id="">
</button>
<p class="story-body-text story-content" data-para-count="165" data-total-count="3909">So Comey chose lordy, that would be bad over catastrophic. He didnt offer much insight into the difference, other than catastrophic sure sounds worse.</p><div id="story-ad-2" class="story-ad ad ad-placeholder nocontent robots-nocontent ">

But he certainly handled the Clinton and Trump investigations in different ways that had profound partisan impacts. But even if he wasnt motivated by partisanship, he showed incredibly bad judgment. For an F.B.I. director, thats disturbing, too.</p><footer class="story-footer story-content">
